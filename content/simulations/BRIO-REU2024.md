## High resolution climatic projections for South West Indian Ocean (BRIO)


### People in charge and affiliation

Marie-Dominique Leroux, Météo-France, Toulouse


### Scientific context and background

The BRIO project for “Building Resilience in the Indian Ocean” aims to develop high-resolution climate projections that will describe the
Sout West Indian Ocean's climate up to the year 2100. The climate models used make it possible to identify long-term trends in temperature,
rainfall, and cyclonic activity in the region.

### Number of simulations (+ number of members if ensemble)

This set includes 19 new-generation models – CMIP6. It includes regional simulations from the CNRM-ESM2.1 / ALADIN63 model pair
and 18 simulations corrected directly from GCMs (statistical downscaling).


### Configurations, parameters, resolution, timestep, domain, span

The climatic regional model ALADIN-v6 at 12km resolution is coupled to a global CMIP6 model, called CNRM-ESM2-A with ~150km resolution
The historical period convers 1981-2014 and projections are for 2015-2100

### Target variables

Surface temperature, precipitation, infrared radiations, humidity and wind speed
